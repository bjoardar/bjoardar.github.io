Biresh Kumar Joardar finished his PhD from Washington State University in 2020. He is currently an NSF-sponsored Post-doctoral Computing Innovation Fellow (CI-Fellow) at the Department of Electrical and Computer Engineering at Duke University. His current research interests include machine learning, manycore architectures, accelerators for deep learning, hardware reliability and security. He is currently working on developing reliable ReRAM-based AI accelerators and on hardware security. He received the ‘Outstanding Graduate Student Researcher Award’ at Washington state University in 2019. His works have been nominated for Best Paper Awards at prestigious conferences such as DATE and NOCS. He is a member of the IEEE.
